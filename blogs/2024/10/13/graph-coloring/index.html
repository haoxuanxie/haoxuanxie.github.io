Graph coloring with densest subgraph

2024-10-13

Problem definition

An undirected graph GGG is kkk-coloring if all vertices could be colored with at most kkk different colors, such that all adjacent vertices have different colors. For example, a kkk-clique is kkk-coloring, and a tree is 2-coloring. The densest subgraph of a graph GGG, is an induced subgraph of a vertex subset SSS of GGG with nSn_SnS​ vertices and mSm_SmS​ edges, such that its density (i.e., ρmax=2mSnS\rho_{max}=\frac{2m_S}{n_S}ρmax​=nS​2mS​​) is highest among all induced subgraphs. In this article, we aim to prove that for a graph GGG whose densest subgraph has a density ρmax\rho_{max}ρmax​, it is ⌈ρmax+1⌉\lceil\rho_{max}+1\rceil⌈ρmax​+1⌉-coloring.

Proof

Let G=(V,E)G=(V,E)G=(V,E) and n=∣V∣,m=∣E∣n=|V|,m=|E|n=∣V∣,m=∣E∣. We prove it by induction. When n=1n=1n=1, ρmax=0\rho_{max}=0ρmax​=0 and the graph is 1-coloring.

Assume we have proved the case for n=in=in=i. For any graph GGG such that n=i+1n=i+1n=i+1, we denote SSS as the vertex set of its densest subgraph and assume its density is ρmax\rho_{max}ρmax​. Then we remove the smallest degree vertex uuu of GGG and derives a graph with iii vertices, which is proved to be ⌈ρmax′+1⌉\lceil\rho_{max}'+1\rceil⌈ρmax′​+1⌉-coloring and ρmax′\rho_{max}'ρmax′​ is the largest density of the new graph. Obviously, the degree of uuu is no larger than ρmax\rho_{max}ρmax​. Otherwise, the total degree of all vertices in GGG is larger than ρmaxn\rho_{max}nρmax​n, which yields a density larger than ρmax\rho_{max}ρmax​. Therefore, uuu can be colored with at most ⌈ρmax+1⌉\lceil\rho_{max}+1\rceil⌈ρmax​+1⌉ colors (if only considering its neighbours).

We claim that ρmax′≤ρmax\rho_{max}'\leq\rho_{max}ρmax′​≤ρmax​. Then GGG is ⌈ρmax+1⌉\lceil\rho_{max}+1\rceil⌈ρmax​+1⌉-coloring (note that if a graph is aaa-coloring and a≤ba\leq ba≤b, then it is also bbb-coloring).

We prove it by contradiction. If ρmax′>ρmax\rho_{max}'>\rho_{max}ρmax′​>ρmax​, removing uuu results in some induced subgraph with a larger density. However, by definition, the densest subgraph of GGG is already the induced subgraph with the highest density. Therefore, it is impossible to find a denser subgraph by removing a vertex in GGG.

By induction, the proof is done.

Finding a coloring scheme

We recursively remove the smallest degree vertex in GGG. Specifically, we maintain a min-heap storing the degrees of all vertices. Initially, we insert all vertices into the heap. For each round, we extract the minimum degree vertex from the heap and remove the vertex. During the remove process, we need to update the degree of its neighbors (for a simple graph, the new degree of its neighbors dnewd_{new}dnew​ should be dold−1d_{old}-1dold​−1) and adjust the heap. With a binary heap, since extracting each vertex and updating the degree of each vertex is O(log⁡n)O(\log n)O(logn), and there are mmm updates in total, the time complexity would be O(mlog⁡n)O(m\log n)O(mlogn). With a Fibonacci heap, where decrease-key function could be done in O(1)O(1)O(1), the time complexity would reduce to O(m+nlog⁡n)O(m+n\log n)O(m+nlogn).

We record the sequence a1,a2,⋯ ,ana_1,a_2,\cdots,a_na1​,a2​,⋯,an​ of removing vertices, i.e., the algorithm removes a1,a2,⋯ ,ana_1,a_2,\cdots,a_na1​,a2​,⋯,an​ sequentially. To construct the coloring scheme, we reversely consider each vertex: let G′G'G′ be an empty graph. We firstly add ana_nan​ to G′G'G′ and color it with 111. Then we add an−1a_{n-1}an−1​ to G′G'G′ and its corresponding edges in GGG connecting ana_nan​ (if exist). If an,an−1a_n,a_{n-1}an​,an−1​ are connected, we color an−1a_{n-1}an−1​ with 222. Otherwise, it is colored with 111. Similarly, we add an−2a_{n-2}an−2​ to G′G'G′ and its edges to an−1,ana_{n-1},a_nan−1​,an​ if exist, and color an−2a_{n-2}an−2​ with the minimum possible color. Repeat this process until a1a_1a1​ is inserted into GGG and colored. The time complexity of this process is bounded by O(m)O(m)O(m).

Therefore, the total time complexity of the algorithm is bounded by O(m+nlog⁡n)O(m+n\log n)O(m+nlogn), and it is straightforward to prove its correctness using the similar technique in the induction proof.
