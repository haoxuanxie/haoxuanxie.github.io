Maintaining the diameter of a growing tree in O(log n) time

2024-09-03

A tree is a connected undirected graph with nnn nodes and n−1n-1n−1 edges. The diameter of a tree is the largest distance between two nodes in the tree.

We define a growing tree as follows: at each moment, a new node uuu is inserted into the tree, and uuu is connected to some node vvv in the original tree. What is the diameter of the new tree?

Let LLL be the path such that the length of LLL is the diameter of the original tree. A key observation is that if w1,w2w_1,w_2w1​,w2​ are the endpoints of LLL, then for the new tree, the endpoints of the new path for the new diameter must be (u,w1),(u,w2)(u,w_1),(u,w_2)(u,w1​),(u,w2​) or (w1,w2)(w_1,w_2)(w1​,w2​).

The proof is rather simple. The distance between w1,w2w_1,w_2w1​,w2​ is actually the length of LLL. By the definition of the tree diameter, it is also the largest distance between two nodes in the original tree. Since the distances between two nodes do not change in the original and new tree, the only candidate nodes to be the new endpoints could only be u,w1,w2u,w_1,w_2u,w1​,w2​ (for other nodes, since they have the same distances which are not larger than ∣L∣|L|∣L∣, we could just ignore them).

Therefore, to derive the new diamter, we only need to maintain w1,w2w_1,w_2w1​,w2​ for each moment, and compute the distances between (u,w1),(u,w2)(u,w_1),(u,w_2)(u,w1​),(u,w2​) and (w1,w2)(w_1,w_2)(w1​,w2​) after each moment, where the largest distance is the new diameter. The distance between (u,w1)(u,w_1)(u,w1​) could be computed by d(u)+d(w1)−2d(LCA(u,w1))d(u)+d(w_1)-2d(LCA(u,w_1))d(u)+d(w1​)−2d(LCA(u,w1​)), where ddd is the depth of the node and LCALCALCA is the lowest common ancestor of two nodes, and other distances could be computed similarly. The depth of the new node uuu could be maintained in O(1)O(1)O(1) time for each moment. Maintaining and computing LCALCALCA could be done in O(log⁡n)O(\log n)O(logn) time for each moment. As a result, we could maintain the diameter of a growing tree in O(log⁡n)O(\log n)O(logn) time.
