Indian conference visa application in Singapore

This page summarizes my experience applying for an Indian conference visa in Singapore (as a non-Singaporean).

Please treat this as practical reference only. In my experience, actual requirements may differ from what is written on official websites, and officers may request additional documents.

Required documents

BLS International asked me to prepare the following:

  • Original passport (BLS keeps it during processing) and one passport copy
  • Copy of the front and back of your Singapore visa/pass
  • Application form (from the official portal)
  • Conference invitation letter from SIGMOD
  • MEA approval
  • MHA approval
  • Recommendation letter from a Singapore-based organization (e.g., your university). The occupation in this letter should match your visa form. If you are a part-time student with a work pass, be ready to explain the mismatch.
  • Fax form (from the official page)
  • Copy of the front and back of your national ID card
  • Confirmed round-trip flight tickets
  • Confirmed hotel booking with your name on it

Notes:

  1. The High Commission of India advises not buying tickets before visa approval, but the BLS conference visa page asks for confirmed flight tickets.
  2. National ID copy and accommodation proof were not listed in official instructions, but I was asked to provide both. It is safer to prepare them in advance.

Process of submission

There are two BLS International offices in Singapore. I submitted at the Bugis branch:
10 Jln Besar, Unit 14-02/04/05 Sim Lim Tower, Singapore 208787.

Step-by-step:

  1. Visa submission is walk-in only during working days from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM.
  2. At the entrance, you may see two queues (left one short, right one long). Usually, the short queue is for visa applications. If you only see one queue, the visa staff is probably on a break. Wait until they return to the reception.
  3. At reception, staff check your materials and issue a queue number.
  4. Wait until your number is called, then submit documents at the assigned counter for another round of checks.
  5. Pay the required fees. NOTICE: You may be told to pay a compulsory S$15 delivery fee, and no pickup is allowed. But if you insist there is a pickup option, they will allow you to choose and waive the delivery fee. Even if you have paid, you can still ask for a pickup option and get a cash refund for the delivery fee.
  6. In my case, the receipt included:
  • S$14.29 BLS service fee (including S$1.18 GST)
  • S$107 visa fee
  • S$4 additional service fee
  • Total: S$125.29

Other information

  • Some applicants reported being told by the High Commission of India that processing might only start in the last week of May. To reduce risk, avoid planning important travel during the expected processing window.

  • Based on experience from other applicants, withholding passport is strictly unallowed.
